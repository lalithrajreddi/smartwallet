What is the Human Development Index?

The Human Development Index (HDI) is a statistical composite index of life expectancy, education, and per capita income indicators, which are used to rank countries into four tiers of human development.

It was developed by economists Mahbub ul Haq and Amartya Sen, and launched by the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) in 1990. The index emphasizes that people and their capabilities should be the primary measure of a country's development rather than economic growth alone.

Three Dimensions of HDI

  • Long and Healthy Life (Health): Evaluated via life expectancy at birth. It reflects access to healthcare, clean water, safety, and nutrition.
  • Knowledge & Education: Evaluated via a combination of Expected Years of Schooling (for children entering school) and Mean Years of Schooling (for adults aged 25+).
  • Decent Standard of Living (Income): Evaluated via Gross National Income (GNI) per capita adjusted for purchasing power parity (PPP).
